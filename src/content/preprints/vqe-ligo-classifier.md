abstract: "We train a variational quantum classifier on real superconducting hardware (IBM Quantum's ibm_nairobi) to discriminate true binary compact-merger gravitational-wave signals from glitch artefacts in LIGO O3 strain data. Compared to PennyLane lightning.qubit simulator baselines, the on-hardware classifier exhibits a characteristic noise-induced regularization behaviour at moderate depths and a separable performance regime above a threshold qubit count. We present hardware-vs-simulator ablations, the encoding strategy used to map whitened strain windows into amplitude-encoded qubit registers, and a discussion of where this approach fits inside existing matched-filter pipelines."
